# Responsible Social Media Use Checklist for College Students

## Do's
- **Verify before sharing**: Always fact-check information using reliable sources like Google or academic sites to avoid spreading misinformation.
- **Set privacy to private**: Limit posts to friends only and review app permissions regularly to protect personal data.
- **Use positively**: Share content that motivates studies, like coding tips or bioengineering reels, to build a professional network.
- **Take breaks**: Schedule screen-free time (e.g., 1 hour daily) to maintain focus on academics and mental health.
- **Engage respectfully**: Comment thoughtfully, using polite language, and report cyberbullying immediately.

## Don'ts
- **Don't overshare personal info**: Avoid posting exact locations, schedules, or sensitive details like dorm addresses.
- **Don't react impulsively**: Pause 10 minutes before posting emotional content to prevent regrets.
- **Don't ignore security**: Never click suspicious links or use weak passwords—scan URLs with VirusTotal.
- **Don't plagiarize content**: Always credit sources when reposting articles or images.
- **Don't compare excessively**: Limit scrolling time to avoid stress from others' highlight reels; unfollow toxic accounts.
